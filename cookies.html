Cookie Policy

Last Updated: January 1, 2023

At Nestelle Greens, we use cookies and similar tracking technologies to enhance your experience on our website. This Cookie Policy explains what cookies are, how we use them, the types of cookies we use, and how you can manage your cookie preferences. By using our website, you consent to the use of cookies in accordance with this policy.

Understanding Cookies

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your computer or mobile device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently, as well as to provide information to the website owners. Cookies typically contain anonymous information such as a unique identifier, the site name, and some digits and numbers. They allow websites to remember your preferences and tailor content to your interests. Cookies cannot carry viruses or install malware on your device.

2. How We Use Cookies

We use cookies for several purposes: to enable certain functions of our website, to provide analytics, to store your preferences, and to enable personalized content. Cookies help us understand how visitors interact with our website by collecting and reporting information anonymously. This information helps us improve our site structure, content, and user experience. We also use cookies to remember your preferences and settings, such as language preferences. Some cookies are essential for the website to function properly.

3. Types of Cookies We Use

Our website uses several types of cookies: Strictly Necessary Cookies (essential for website functionality), Performance Cookies (collect anonymous information about visitor behavior), Functionality Cookies (remember user preferences), and Targeting/Advertising Cookies (track browsing habits to display relevant ads). We also use session cookies (temporary, deleted when you close your browser) and persistent cookies (remain until deleted or expired). Each type serves a specific purpose in enhancing your experience on our site.

4. First-Party Cookies

First-party cookies are set by our website and can only be read by our site. These include cookies for user authentication, session management, and preference storage. For example, we use first-party cookies to remember if you've visited our site before, your login status, and items you've added to your cart. These cookies are essential for providing core website functionality and cannot be disabled without affecting site performance. We carefully control these cookies and only use them for legitimate business purposes.

5. Third-Party Cookies

Third-party cookies are set by domains other than our website, typically by our partners and service providers. These include analytics cookies (like Google Analytics), advertising cookies, and social media cookies (from platforms like Facebook or Twitter). These cookies allow third-party features or functionality to be provided on or through our website. The parties that set these cookies can recognize your device both when it visits our website and when it visits certain other websites. We carefully select our third-party partners and require them to comply with data protection laws.

6. Essential Cookies

Essential cookies are strictly necessary for the operation of our website. They include cookies that enable you to log into secure areas of our site, use a shopping cart, or make use of e-billing services. These cookies don't gather information about you that could be used for marketing or remembering where you've been on the internet. Without these cookies, services you've asked for cannot be provided. We use these cookies based on our legitimate interest in providing a functional website.

7. Performance Cookies

Performance cookies collect information about how visitors use our website, such as which pages visitors go to most often and if they receive error messages. These cookies don't collect information that identifies a visitor; all information is aggregated and anonymous. We use these cookies to understand how our website is used so we can improve it. The data collected includes the number of visitors, where visitors come from, and pages visited. These cookies help us test new designs and improve site navigation.

8. Functionality Cookies

Functionality cookies allow our website to remember choices you make (such as your username, language preference, or region) and provide enhanced, more personal features. These cookies can also be used to remember changes you've made to text size, fonts, and other customizable elements of web pages. They may also be used to provide services you've asked for such as watching a video or commenting on a blog. The information these cookies collect may be anonymized and they cannot track your browsing activity on other websites.

9. Targeting/Advertising Cookies

Targeting cookies are used to deliver advertisements more relevant to you and your interests. They are also used to limit the number of times you see an advertisement as well as help measure the effectiveness of advertising campaigns. They remember that you've visited our website and this information is shared with other organizations such as advertisers. While these cookies can track your visits to our website and other sites on the internet, they don't usually contain personally identifiable information.

10. Session Cookies

Session cookies are temporary cookies that remain in your browser's cookie file only until you leave our website. These cookies are essential for certain applications or functionality and allow us to link your actions during a particular browser session. They enable you to move from page to page on our website and use its features without having to log in repeatedly. Session cookies are deleted when you close your browser. They don't collect information from your computer and typically store information in the form of a session identification.

Cookie Management

11. Cookie Consent

When you first visit our website, you'll see a cookie consent banner that allows you to accept or decline non-essential cookies. Your consent preferences will be stored in a cookie so we can remember your choices on subsequent visits. You can change your cookie preferences at any time through our cookie preference center. Essential cookies don't require consent as they're necessary for basic website functionality. We'll only set non-essential cookies if you provide explicit consent through our banner or preference center.

12. Browser Cookie Controls

Most web browsers allow some control of most cookies through browser settings. You can set your browser to refuse all or some browser cookies, or to alert you when websites set or access cookies. If you disable or refuse cookies, please note that some parts of our website may become inaccessible or not function properly. The methods for managing cookies vary by browser and device. You can usually find these settings in the 'Options' or 'Preferences' menu of your browser. Popular browsers like Chrome, Firefox, and Safari provide detailed cookie management options.

13. Opting Out of Analytics

To opt out of being tracked by Google Analytics across all websites, you can install the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on. This add-on prevents the Google Analytics JavaScript from sharing information with Google Analytics about your visit activity. For other analytics services we use, you'll need to check their respective opt-out mechanisms. Some services provide opt-out cookies that you can set to prevent tracking. Remember that if you clear your cookies, you may need to reset these opt-out preferences.

14. Advertising Opt-Out

Many advertising networks allow you to opt out of targeted advertising. In the US, you can visit the Network Advertising Initiative (NAI) opt-out page or the Digital Advertising Alliance (DAA) opt-out page. In Europe, you can visit the European Interactive Digital Advertising Alliance (EDAA) opt-out page. These opt-outs are often cookie-based and will only work for the specific browser you're using when you set them. You'll need to repeat the opt-out process for each browser and device you use to access our site.

15. Mobile Device Controls

On mobile devices, you may have additional options to control how cookies and other tracking technologies are used. Both iOS and Android provide settings to limit ad tracking. These settings affect how advertisers use cookies and other tracking methods to serve you targeted ads. However, they don't necessarily block all tracking cookies. For comprehensive cookie control on mobile devices, you may need to use browser settings in addition to device settings. Some mobile browsers also offer private browsing modes that limit cookie storage and tracking.
